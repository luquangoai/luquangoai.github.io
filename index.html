About Me

I am Quang Oai (Bill), a PhD candidate in Environmental and Occupational Health (EOH) at National Cheng Kung University (NCKU), Taiwan, advised by Prof. Lee Ching Chang. My research focuses on environmental health, air quality monitoring, and predictive model development, with applications in exposure assessment, health risk evaluation, and environmental epidemiology.

I have developed the G2I-En model for indoor air quality and personal exposure assessment, and investigated emerging contaminants such as OPFRs, PBDEs, PFAS, and PAEs in river systems. My work also extends to hydrological modeling and urban flood mitigation, reflecting a broader interest in environmental systems and public health resilience.

I have published in leading journals and conferences, including EI, EP, Indoor Air (2022, 2024), AAAR 2025, and TSIEQ 2024, with Citations156 h-index7 i10-index6 .

Selected Papers

Q1 2026 IF 7.9 Review Article
Unveiling the Hidden Gap of PM2.5 Exposure: A Critical Review of Bridging Perspectives from Indoor and Outdoor Predictive Models
Quang-Oai Lu, Chang W.H., Chen H.L., Lee C.C.
Results in Engineering, 29, 109830
Our review synthesizes evidence on how PM2.5 moves between indoor and outdoor environments, showing that relying on outdoor measurements alone can substantially misclassify personal exposure. We evaluate spatial models, CTMs, machine learning, and infiltration models, highlighting that hybrid “bridging” ensembled models coupling outdoor fields with building characteristics and infiltration dynamics provide the most realistic basis for exposure assessment. Future priorities include composition-aware metrics, high-resolution modelling, and federated/physics-informed AI.

Q1 2025 IF 8.1 Research Article
Innovative Geo-AI Model: An Enhancement of Outdoor PM Estimations Based on Land Use and Outdoor Environmental Factors in a Highly Polluted Area
Quang-Oai Lu, Lee C.C.
Chemosphere, 373, 144178
Using daily PM2.5 and PM10 measurements from EPA stations, AirBox sensors, and micro-stations in highly polluted Taiwan (2018–2020), we built an ensemble Geo-AI framework integrating LUR, IDW, SVM, random forests, and MLPs. The model achieved R²=0.89 for PM2.5 and R²=0.87 for PM10, outperforming individual models. This is the first LUR-based application in Southern Taiwan, providing a scientific foundation for urban planning and public health policy.

Q1 2024 IF 8.9 Research Article
Enhancing Indoor PM2.5 Predictions Based on Land Use and Indoor Environmental Factors by Applying Machine Learning and Spatial Modeling Approaches
Quang-Oai Lu, Chang W.H., Chu H.J., Lee C.C.
Environmental Pollution, 363, 125093
Since individuals spend ~90% of their time indoors, indoor PM2.5 is a critical yet underestimated component of total exposure. From 102 residential households in Taiwan, our framwork achieved a cross-validated R²=0.92 and RMSE=2.3 µg/m³ — with outdoor PM2.5, infiltration factor, and cleaning frequency as the most influential predictors. This is one of the first studies to integrate both geospatial outdoor estimates and detailed indoor environmental factors into a unified prediction framework for IAQ. The resulting model has direct applications for estimating real individual PM exposure in large cohorts and provides actionable insights for IAQ management and urban health policy.

Q1 2023 IF 10.4 Research Article
Investigating the associations between organophosphate flame retardants (OPFRs) and fine particles in paired indoor and outdoor air: A probabilistic prediction model for deriving OPFRs in indoor environments
Quang-Oai Lu, Jung, C.C., Chao, H.W., et al.
Environment International, 176, 107871
This study measured 11 OPFRs and PM₂.₅ in 178 paired indoor–outdoor air samples from 89 children's homes in southern Taiwan. Indoor OPFR concentrations were generally higher than outdoor levels, with indoor/outdoor ratios exceeding 1 for most compounds, indicating predominantly indoor sources. Apartments showed higher OPFR burdens than houses due to more furnishings and electronics. A probabilistic prediction model achieved R²=0.87, identifying mattress materials, air purifiers, heaters, and total interior material area as key determinants.

Q1 2022 IF 7.6 Research Article
Seasonal and source characteristics of organophosphorus flame retardants in air and house dust in Taiwan residential microenvironments: Implications for young children's exposure and risk assessment using a probabilistic approach
Quang-Oai Lu, Jung, C.C., Liu, Y.H., Chang, W.H.
Environmental Pollution, 308, 120893
This study measured 11 OPFRs in indoor air and house dust from bedrooms and living rooms in Taiwanese homes to characterise seasonal patterns, sources, and young children's exposure. Indoor air OPFR concentrations were higher in bedrooms and in the warm season; dust levels were higher in the cold season. Using a Monte Carlo probabilistic approach, average daily exposure doses via inhalation and dust ingestion were similar between preschool and school-aged children. Overall, OPFR exposure represents acceptable non-carcinogenic and negligible carcinogenic risk.
