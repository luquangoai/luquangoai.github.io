Research Overview

My research centers on environmental health and air quality monitoring, with a particular focus on developing predictive models for air pollutants and evaluating their impact on public health. By integrating historical emission data, field sampling, and laboratory analysis, I work to improve understanding of pollutant dynamics in indoor and outdoor environments — notably through the creation of the G2I-En model for personal indoor air quality (IAQ) exposure assessment.

I have also explored emerging contaminants — including organophosphate flame retardants (OPFRs), polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDEs), PFAS, and phthalates (PAEs) — in river systems, linking environmental monitoring with health risk evaluation and epidemiology. Beyond air quality, my work extends to hydrological modeling and urban flood mitigation, aiming to inform environmental policy and support next-generation air monitoring solutions.
